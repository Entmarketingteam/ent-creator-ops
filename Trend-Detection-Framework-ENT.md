## The Core Problem

By the time a product trends on TikTok and explodes on Amazon, the creator window is already closing. The goal is to find trends **at Stage 1-2**, not Stage 3-4:

```
Stage 1: Niche communities discover it (Reddit, K-beauty forums, PubMed)
Stage 2: Early creators post → Google Trends starts rising → Amazon searches begin
Stage 3: TikTok virality → Amazon search spikes → Brands scramble for creators ← Most creators enter here
Stage 4: Saturated, CPCs spike, brands have locked contracts ← Too late
```

**Creatine for women** was Stage 2 in early 2024. PDRN was Stage 2 in late 2024. Right now they're Stage 3.
**This framework finds Stage 1-2 before others do.**

## Tool 1: Google Trends — The Earliest Signal

Google Trends shows consumer curiosity *before* purchase intent forms on Amazon. A topic trending on Google will hit Amazon 4-12 weeks later.

### Searches to run weekly

**Ingredient/product searches:**
- Go to trends.google.com → Search the ingredient/product → Set to "Past 12 months"
- Look for a J-curve in the last 60-90 days (interest was flat, now rising)
- Check "Related queries" tab → sort by **Rising** → Anything marked **Breakout (5000%+)** = Stage 1 signal

**Category-level searches:**
Search broad categories and look at the "Related topics" and "Related queries" for breakout sub-terms:
- `skincare ingredients` → what's rising in related queries?
- `supplements for women` → what new ingredient is appearing?
- `K-beauty` → what ingredient is appearing that isn't mainstream yet?
- `gut health` → what sub-category is rising?

**Geographic arbitrage — biggest edge:**
Look for trends that are **huge in Korea, Australia, or UK but still small in US**.
These hit the US 6-18 months later.
- Filter: Compare **South Korea / Australia / United Kingdom vs. United States**
- If an ingredient is at 70-100 in Korea but 5-15 in US → US wave is coming
- This is exactly how PDRN, COSRX, Medicube, GHK-CU played out

### Trend fingerprint on Google Trends
| Signal | What it looks like | Stage |
|--------|-------------------|-------|
| Interest score 5-20, rising | Niche curiosity forming | 1 |
| Interest score 20-50, J-curve starting | Early mainstream | 2 ← **TARGET ZONE** |
| Interest score 50-80, sustained | Mid-trend | 3 |
| Interest score 80-100, peaked | Late/saturated | 4 |

## Tool 2: Helium 10 — Amazon Entry Signal

When Google Trends shows Stage 1-2, use Helium 10 to confirm the trend is entering Amazon. These specific tools in order:

### Step 1: Magnet (Keyword Research)
Enter the seed keyword → look at the **Trend** column (the sparkline).

**What to look for:**
- Search volume: **500-15,000/mo** (below mainstream radar, above noise)
- Trend sparkline: **flat then sharply rising in last 60-90 days** = money signal
- CPC: **rising** even as volume is still low = brands are entering, validating the category
- Related keywords section: Look for adjacent terms also rising → confirms a category forming, not just one product

**Filters to set:**
- Monthly search volume: 300 – 30,000
- Search volume trend: Positive (trending up)
- Competition: Low-Medium (many competitors = late; 0-3 = too early)

### Step 2: Cerebro (Reverse ASIN)
Find the top 3-5 products in the emerging category. Run them through Cerebro.

**What to look for:**
- Keywords that ALL products rank for = core category vocabulary
- Under "Search Volume Trend" — look for keywords where volume has doubled in 90 days
- Look at **Organic Rank changes** — if multiple products are climbing rankings organically on the same keywords, consumer demand is pulling them up (not just paid ads pushing them)
- **New keywords** (appearing in last 90 days) that didn't exist before = emerging vocabulary

**The COSRX / Medicube pattern:** When 3+ similar products all start ranking for the same new ingredient keyword AND that keyword is trending up = ingredient category forming on Amazon.

### Step 3: Trendster (Search Volume Over Time)
Enter any keyword → see the full 2-year search history.

**The "hockey stick" pattern:**
- 18 months of flat/low volume (200-2,000 searches)
- Then a sharp upward turn in the last 3-6 months
- This is the exact pattern for: creatine (women's), K18, PDRN, GHK-CU

If you see the hockey stick *just starting* (the curve is only 30-60 days into the upturn) → Stage 2. Maximum window for creator/brand action.

### Step 4: Black Box (Product Discovery)
Use Black Box to find the *products* benefiting from the trend:

**Filter settings for emerging category discovery:**
- Monthly Revenue: $15,000 – $400,000 (too low = no market; too high = saturated)
- Review Count: Under 500 (new entrant)
- BSR: Trending down (improving rank = growing sales)
- Date launched: Last 12-24 months
- Category: the relevant top-level category

Products that appear here with <500 reviews but strong revenue = early winners. The brands selling these are the ones to pitch for partnerships.

## Tool 3: SmartScout — Momentum Confirmation

Once Google Trends + Helium 10 confirm a signal, SmartScout tells you it's real and gives you the category-level view.

### Search Terms Explorer — Weekly Pull Cadence
**The most important SmartScout workflow:**

Export the Search Terms Explorer weekly for your target categories. Build a comparison spreadsheet. The key metric is **week-over-week delta on monthly change %**.

**Filter settings:**
- Monthly Change %: **> 15%** (signals building momentum)
- Search Volume: **1,000 – 50,000** (below mainstream, above noise)
- Sort by: Monthly Change % descending

**The sweet spot zone:** A term at 5,000-20,000 searches with +20-40% monthly change is exactly where creatine, PDRN, and K18 were before they became household names.

**Red flags (skip these):**
- Terms with 200,000+ searches and 5% change = mature, competitive, late
- Terms with <500 searches but 50%+ change = noise, not signal
- Purely seasonal spikes (Easter ribbon, holiday terms) = not a trend

### Category Revenue Tracking
In SmartScout's Category tools, track:
- **New brand market share** — When brands launched <2 years ago are capturing 15%+ of category revenue, the category is in transition. This is when new creator partnerships win.
- **Brand concentration** — A category where the top 3 brands have <50% share = fragmented = brand battle = brands need creator amplification NOW
- Look at categories where the **#1 brand is a legacy/mass brand but #3-10 are DTC challenger brands** — the challengers are hungry for creator deals

### Trending Terms Export — Category-by-Category
Export trending terms for each category separately, weekly:
1. Health & Household → supplements, personal care, hair care
2. Beauty & Personal Care → skincare, makeup, fragrance
3. Sports & Outdoors → fitness equipment, activewear, recovery
4. Grocery → functional foods, beverages, snacks

Track which terms are **newly appearing** each week (not in last week's export). New entries with >10K volume = fast-moving consumer interest.

## Weekly Monitoring SOP

### Monday: Google Trends (30 min)
1. Check all items on the Longer-Term Radar list
2. Run "Compare" on 3-4 ingredients vs. each other to see which is accelerating fastest
3. Check Geographic: Korea, Australia, UK vs. US for beauty/skincare terms
4. Screenshot anything showing J-curve. Log to tracking spreadsheet.

### Wednesday: SmartScout Export (20 min)
1. Export Search Terms Explorer for: Health & Household, Beauty, Sports, Grocery
2. Filter: >15% monthly change, 1K-50K volume
3. Compare to last week's export — note any NEW entries
4. Flag any terms crossing from <10% to >20% change (momentum building)

### Thursday: Helium 10 Check (30 min)
1. Any flagged Google Trends signals → run through Magnet + Trendster
2. Any new SmartScout terms → Cerebro the top products in that search result
3. Black Box scan: Beauty + Health categories, revenue $15K-$300K, reviews <400, launched <18mo
4. Log hockey-stick Trendster charts

### Monthly: Exploding Topics Pull (1 hr)
1. Export all consumer-relevant categories
2. Filter: "Exponential" speed + "Growing" forecast + growth >40%
3. Cross-reference with your active watchlist — anything moving from Radar → Medium → High confidence?
4. Update this framework with new watchlist entries
