The skincare audience has been conditioned to build routines, layer actives, and buy quality — but they've been applying that logic exclusively to their face. The "treat your body like your face" angle is the unlock. It's not a new behavior, it's an extension of an existing one. This is Stage 2 territory — mainstream enough to have purchase intent, early enough that there's minimal creator competition.

## Content Series: "Treating My Body Like My Face"

### Piece 1 — "My Full Body Care Routine"
**Format:** TikTok/Reel, 60-75 sec — process tutorial, high saves
**Hook type:** Routine reveal — the format that drives saves and repeat traffic

**Script framework:**
> "I've been treating my body the same way I treat my face, and the difference is insane."
>
> "Here's my full body routine — step by step:"
>
> **In the shower:**
> "Step 1 — [PH-balancing body wash]. Most body washes are too alkaline. This one maintains your skin's natural barrier. I use [brand]."
>
> "Step 2 — Exfoliating 2x a week. [Product — exfoliating glove or scrub]. Same reason you exfoliate your face — removes dead skin, helps everything absorb better."
>
> **Out of the shower (damp skin):**
> "Step 3 — Body oil first. [Dry body oil product]. Apply to damp skin — it traps the moisture in instead of sitting on top."
>
> "Step 4 — Niacinamide body lotion on top. [Product]. Niacinamide on your body works the same way it does on your face: fades dark spots, smooths texture, evens skin tone. I use it on my arms and legs."
>
> "Step 5 (2-3x/week): Body retinol or glycolic acid on rough patches — elbows, knees, back of arms. The actives that changed my face work on your body too."
>
> "Everything is linked in my storefront under 'My Body Care Routine.'"

**Caption:** "Treating my body like my face — full routine 🤍 every product linked in storefront #bodycare #skincareroutine #bodyskincare #amazonfind #niacinamide"
